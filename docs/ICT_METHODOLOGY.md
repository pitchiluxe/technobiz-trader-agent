# ICT Methodology Guide

## Overview

The Inner Circle Trading (ICT) methodology by Michael S is a systematic approach to identifying high-probability trade entries using institutional-level concepts.

## Core Components

### 1. Liquidity Sweep (Smart Money Manipulation)
- **Concept**: Price moves beyond recent swing highs/lows to capture retail stops
- **Purpose**: Smart money creates liquidity before moving in the intended direction
- **Identification**: 
  - Price breaks above recent resistance or below recent support
  - Often creates false breakouts
  - Followed by rapid reversal back into previous range
- **Trading Use**: Enter on reversal after liquidity is swept

### 2. Break of Structure (BoS)
- **Concept**: Price breaks previous swing patterns
- **Uptrend BoS**: Lower low breaks below recent swing low
- **Downtrend BoS**: Higher high breaks above recent swing high
- **Purpose**: Signals potential trend change or continuation
- **Trading Use**: Confluence signal for entry confirmation

### 3. Imbalance (Fair Value Gap)
- **Concept**: Unfilled area in price action where no trading occurred
- **Appearance**: Gap between candles on a chart
- **Purpose**: Price acts as a magnet and tends to fill imbalances
- **Trading Use**: Target imbalance areas for entry or take profit levels

### 4. Order Block
- **Concept**: Area where institutional traders entered/exited large positions
- **Identification**: Strong rejection candle or price stall before a move
- **Purpose**: Price retests order blocks for re-entry or continuation
- **Trading Use**: Support/Resistance levels with high-probability rebounds

### 5. Kill Zone (Execution Window)
- **Definition**: 15-30 minute window after order block identification
- **Significance**: Prime time when price retraces into the block for entry
- **Execution**: Best entries occur when price pulls back into kill zone
- **Management**: Signal expires if kill zone closes without entry

## Trading Process Using ICT

1. **Identify Liquidity Sweep**
   - Spot where price broke swing levels
   - Confirm reversal away from extreme

2. **Confirm Break of Structure**
   - Lower low in uptrend (or higher high in downtrend)
   - Validates potential entry setup

3. **Mark Imbalance/Order Block**
   - Note unfilled gaps or rejection zones
   - These become entry or profit target areas

4. **Wait for Pullback (Kill Zone)**
   - Price retraces toward order block
   - Enter during this pullback window
   - Use imbalance as stop loss reference

## Example Setup

**EURUSD Bullish Setup:**
- Swing Low at 1.0480
- Liquidity Sweep: Price breaks above 1.0520 (false breakout)
- BoS: Price breaks below 1.0480 with lower low
- Order Block: Rejection candle at 1.0500
- Imbalance: Gap between 1.0500-1.0515
- Kill Zone Entry: Price retests 1.0505 (within order block)
- Entry: 1.0510
- Stop Loss: 1.0480 (below order block)
- Take Profit: 1.0580, 1.0650

## Risk Management in ICT Trading

- **Position Size**: 2% of account per trade
- **Risk/Reward**: Minimum 1:2 ratio (1 risk : 2 potential reward)
- **Stop Loss**: Always placed beyond order block
- **Profit Targets**: Multiple levels for partial exits
- **Drawdown Limit**: 5% daily maximum

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Trading without confirmation of all four elements
❌ Ignoring time frames analysis
❌ Trading during low liquidity periods
❌ Entering after kill zone has passed
❌ Not respecting hard stop losses
❌ Over-leveraging positions

## Multi-Timeframe Confluence

**Best Entries Occur When:**
- Daily timeframe shows clear uptrend/downtrend
- 4-hour timeframe confirms order block/imbalance
- 1-hour timeframe marks kill zone entry opportunity
- All three confluence: Entry probability increases significantly
